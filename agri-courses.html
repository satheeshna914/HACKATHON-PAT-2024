AGRICULTURAL TRAINING

1. Rice farming involves cultivating rice paddies flooded with water, primarily in Asia. It's a labor-intensive process, with planting, maintaining, and harvesting being crucial stages. Rice serves as a staple food for over half the world's population, making it vital for global food security.

2. Watermelon cropping entails planting watermelon seeds in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. It requires regular watering, especially during the fruiting stage. Farmers must manage pests and diseases. Harvesting occurs when the watermelons reach maturity, typically by tapping and observing the sound. Watermelons are enjoyed worldwide for their sweet, refreshing taste.

3. Peanut cropping involves cultivating peanuts, also known as groundnuts, primarily in warm climates. The crop requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Farmers plant peanuts directly into the soil, where they develop underground. Harvesting involves uprooting the entire plant and extracting the peanuts, which are rich in protein and oil, making them valuable commodities.
